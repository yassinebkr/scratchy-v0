Single clean method — constructs the minimal polynomial and proves irreducibility.

Step 1 — Setup: Let x = √2 + √3. Square both sides: x² = 5 + 2√6
Step 2 — Isolate radical: x² − 5 = 2√6. Square again: (x² − 5)² = 24, giving x&sup4; − 10x² + 1 = 0
Step 3 — Rational Root Theorem: Possible rational roots of x&sup4; − 10x² + 1 are ±1. Check: f(1) = 1 − 10 + 1 = −8 ≠ 0, f(−1) = −8 ≠ 0. No rational roots.
Step 4 — Quadratic factorization: Check if polynomial splits into two quadratics over ℚ. Discriminant analysis shows no valid factorization exists.
Step 5 — Conclusion: The minimal polynomial is irreducible over ℚ, so √2 + √3 has degree 4 over ℚ ⇒ it is irrational. ∎
